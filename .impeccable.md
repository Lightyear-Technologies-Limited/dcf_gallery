# DCF Gallery

A digital gallery for the Digital Culture Fund (DCF), a Hivemind initiative
collecting, contextualizing, and preserving culturally significant digital art.
311 works by 11 artists across ~17 visible collections.

## Design Context

### Users

**Primary**: Art collectors and advisors evaluating DCF's holdings as a thesis. They
arrive with conviction about digital art and want to see the works treated with
museum-grade respect.

**Secondary**: Fund stakeholders / LPs auditing the holdings as a coherent
position. They want institutional clarity - chain, contract, edition, holdings
count - not marketplace energy.

**Tertiary**: Journalists, curators, first-time visitors exploring digital art
broadly. They need wayfinding and a clear thesis but should not feel pandered to.

The site is consumed in a calm context - desktop, daylight, considered scrolling.
Not at a poker table, not in transit. The reader leans in.

### Brand Personality

Three words: **considered, institutional, restrained**.

**Voice**: Editorial. Sentences are long enough to mean something. Numbers are
precise (DCF holds 36 of 1,000) and presented in tabular figures. Names are
spelled correctly (a.c.k. with the periods, not ACK). The fund speaks AS the fund,
not as a marketplace listing it.

**Emotional goal**: The visitor should feel they have entered a space where the
work matters and the curator knows why. No urgency, no "trending", no "floor
price". Quiet authority.

### Aesthetic Direction

**References**: Gagosian, Pace, David Zwirner, White Cube. Hauser & Wirth's online
catalogue. The Met's collection pages. Magazine catalogue raisonné aesthetic.

**Anti-references**: OpenSea, Blur, generic NFT marketplaces. Any site whose
homepage opens with a stat block. Any "Inter / Plus Jakarta / Space Grotesk on a
gradient hero" interface. Any site that announces "AI-curated" or "trending".

**Theme**: Light-mode default - eggshell `#F8F8F7`, warm-tinted near-black
foreground. The eggshell is non-negotiable; it was specifically chosen as
museum-worthy and Pace-inspired. Dark mode supported as a preference but not the
default.

**Type**: Argent Thin (Klim) for display sizes - 48-72px, tight tracking. Instrument
Sans for UI and body. Note: Instrument Sans is in the impeccable reflex-rejects
list, but its use here is a deliberate brand decision predating this skill, paired
with Argent which carries the distinctive weight. Do not swap it.

### Design Principles

1. **Typography is hierarchy, not color.** Size and weight do the work. Accent
   colors are for chapter labels only; everything else is grayscale on warm
   neutrals.

2. **Whitespace is currency. What's absent defines premium.** 80-120px section
   gaps are right. Tight spacing is for caption pairs. Never compress to gain
   density.

3. **Images never have text overlays.** Captions live below. No gradient overlays,
   no hover-reveal text, no badge corners.

4. **Navigation is text.** A fixed left sidebar with the H-mark and three links.
   No icons in nav.

5. **The homepage is curated, not queried.** A single random hero per page-load.
   Salon-wall sections by artist. No search, no filters as default. Filtering is
   secondary, not the primary surface.

6. **The art is the interface.** Anywhere I'm tempted to add a control or
   decoration, ask if removing it would hurt - if not, remove it.

7. **Numbers are tabular.** Token IDs, counts, contract addresses, edition
   ratios - all `font-mono tabular-nums`. The fund speaks in figures.

8. **Eyebrow labels are 10px tracked uppercase.** "ARTIST", "CHAPTER", "HIVEMIND
   COMMENTARY", "DIGITAL CULTURE FUND" - the institutional voice.

9. **Motion is functional, not decorative.** `transition-colors duration-200` on
   interactive surfaces is OK; `transition-transform`, scale flourishes, parallax,
   and entrance animations are not.

10. **Every surface earns its place.** No cards-inside-cards. No gradient borders.
    No glassmorphism. No drop shadows beyond what a real wall mount would cast (which
    is none, in a virtual gallery).
